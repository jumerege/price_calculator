💡 Understanding Volumetric Billing

What is Volumetric Billing?

Think of it like shipping a very light but very large package. If you ship a huge pillow that weighs only 1 kg but takes up 100 liters of space, the shipping company might charge you based on the SPACE it takes, not just the weight.

How Does It Work?

The calculator checks two things:

  1. Actual Weight: How much your payload actually weighs
  2. Space Charge: What the space it occupies is worth in weight

Then we charge you for whichever is HIGHER.

What is the "Volumetric Factor"?

The Volumetric Factor is how we convert space into weight equivalent.

Default value: 2 (L/kg)

This means: 2 liters of space = 1 kg of billable weight

Real Example 🎯

Scenario: You send a balloon full of sensors

  • 🎈 Actual weight: 2 kg
  • 📦 Space it takes: 100 liters
  • ⚙️ Volumetric Factor: 2 L/kg

Calculation:

Space Charge = 100 liters ÷ 2 = 50 kg equivalent
You pay for = max(2 kg actual, 50 kg equivalent) = 50 kg ✈️

Result: You get charged like if your balloon weighed 50 kg!

Another Example: Heavy Object 🏋️

Scenario: You send a small dense instrument

  • 🔧 Actual weight: 60 kg
  • 📦 Space it takes: 50 liters
  • ⚙️ Volumetric Factor: 2 L/kg

Calculation:

Space Charge = 50 liters ÷ 2 = 25 kg equivalent
You pay for = max(60 kg actual, 25 kg equivalent) = 60 kg ✈️

Result: You get charged for actual weight (60 kg) because it's heavier!

Can I Change It?

Yes! Higher Volumetric Factor = cheaper for bulky items

  • Factor 2 (default): More space-efficient
  • Factor 3: Bulkier items get charged less
  • Factor 4: Very bulky items are even cheaper
